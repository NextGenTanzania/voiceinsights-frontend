# v182.1 — Export Quality & Public Polish Patch QA

## Scope
Patch-only update for public report exports, export labels, public wording, track-download safety, and visible infographic polish. No homepage, brand identity, navigation, auth, schema, or unrelated dashboards were changed.

## Manual QA Checklist

### Browser visual QA
- Open `/sample-reports.html` in a normal browser session.
- Confirm cards use client-facing labels and no internal phase names.
- Open at least Health, Agriculture, Humanitarian, Annual Impact, and SDG samples.
- Confirm the report viewer shows Executive Intelligence View, executive brief, decision dashboard, risk dashboard, methodology, evidence dashboard, and infographic pages.

### Mobile QA
- Test at 390px, 768px, and desktop width.
- Confirm infographic pages collapse to one column.
- Confirm export toolbar remains usable.

### Print/PDF QA
- Click Print-ready report.
- Confirm assistant/actions are hidden in print.
- Confirm infographic pages do not break awkwardly and have evidence labels.

### Export QA
- Download Executive intelligence brief, Donor impact brief, Policy brief preview, Infographic report preview, Statistical annex preview, Dataset appendix preview, and Presentation talking points.
- Confirm output is readable, client-facing, and does not appear as raw JSON.
- Confirm labels say preview/outline where true PDF/PPTX generation is not used.

### Public wording QA
Search visible public output for:
- `Not enough data`
- `placeholder`
- `generated by system`
- `raw JSON`
- `undefined`
- `null`
- `NaN`
- `VRDS Showcase Experience`
- `Phase 20`
- `Structured assistant actions`

None should appear in user-visible report pages or export previews.

### Route safety QA
- Confirm `/api/public/demo-reports/:id/track-download` only increments rows where `is_demo=1 AND status='published'`.
- Confirm non-demo report IDs are not exposed through public demo routes.

## Known remaining risk
Exports are still export previews / browser print flows rather than a fully server-rendered PDF/PPTX engine. Do not market them as final server-side PDF/PPTX generation until a dedicated export engine exists.
