Voice-to-evidence pipeline

Every stage produces a reviewable output.

Collect, validate, process and analyze first; then monitor, publish, govern the evidence and record accountable decisions. Stage numbers and output labels preserve the complete sequence without relying on connector colour.

  1. Collect

    Phone, WhatsApp, SMS, web and offline inputs.

    Output Source record
  2. Validate

    Consent, completeness and supervisor review.

    Output Approved response
  3. Process

    Transcription, language handling and structuring.

    Output Structured evidence
  4. Analyze

    Indicators, themes, patterns and maps.

    Output Analysis model
  5. Monitor

    Dashboards, quality, progress and risks.

    Output Monitoring view
  6. Report & Publish

    Reports, publications, datasets and briefs.

    Output Governed data product
  7. Govern Evidence

    Sources, methodology, metadata and versions.

    Output Evidence chain
  8. Decide

    Actions, owners, deadlines and impact.

    Output Decision record

Common questions

Practical answers before implementation.

Scope, channels and support are confirmed during discovery and reflected in the approved implementation plan.

Can field teams work offline?

Supported offline workflows store assigned work locally and synchronize through controlled processes when connectivity returns.

Who controls the data?

Ownership, permitted processing, retention and portability are defined in the applicable customer agreement and implementation scope.

Which downloads are available?

Approved PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Excel, CSV and JSON outputs are shown only where the current governed workflow supports them.

How is the platform implemented?

Discovery, workflow design, configuration, training, pilot validation and launch are scoped to the organization.

What support is included?

Training, operational support and service levels are defined in the tailored proposal rather than assumed as a universal package.
